Adventures in Nairen

Neiren (nigh-ren) is a small farming village just a few miles south of the Dyossis Jungle and part of the Duchy of Haderek. It is managed on behalf of its lord by a local bailiff. Sheriff Anissa, known for her sour attitude towards adventurers, is responsible for keeping law and order in the village while Forester Cale does the same for the lumber camps in the jungle.

Situated beyond the sheltering protection of Haderek’s Great Wall, Neiren faces dangers from the nearby Dyossis Jungle and Bog of Kresh, goblins foremost among them. Thanks to the presence of powerful residents, such as the wizard Silion, the village itself is protected, but in the Outer Homesteads, and especially the lumber camps, goblin raids are frequent.

The Duchy of Haderek is an influential province in the Kingdom of Den, and its exports can be found in all three kingdoms which make up the Empire of the Three-Fold Throne. Constantly under threat from creatures of the Dyossis Jungle to the north, Haderek has commissioned the construction of a massive wall that will, upon completion, stretch more than 500 miles and shelter the entire northern border.

Our adventurers each found themselves heading to this small hommlet for their own reasons.

Part One: The Alrose Affair - ca 0AG

Heading west along the road from Two-Bridges, Rhogar and Balec came upon an overturned wagon, and rushed toward the sound of a child crying for help. Meanwhile, Faylen and Starling were headed south from the jungle, but immediately recognized the scene for what it was, a trap set by goblins. There was a brief melee before the goblins were driven off or killed and the two pairs of adventurers realized they were each seeking the same wizard in the same small village.

Upon arrival at the village of Nairen they saw smoke rising from one of the outer homesteads. Hoping to help, they ventured to the smoke and found the home of the Alrose family in ruin, several members dead. They were able to save one member and capture a small goblin called Krenurt, and learned that the farm had been attacked by giants and goblins looking for a book. They returned to the village where they learned Silion was out of town.

Not content to sit idly they investigated the events at the Alrose farm more closely, ultimately learning that the giants were receiving intelligence from a lumberjack called onethumb. They found him and learned that the giants had attacked the Alrose farm by mistake, and that they were currently headed to the Elerose farm. Once again, the adventurers intervened, finding a giant and a small group of goblins in the process of raiding the homestead. After a very hard-fought battle against the giant, they emerged victorious. They found a small lockbox containing a mask and robe associated with a cult, but no book. They returned to town to await Silion's return.

Part Two: The Folly of Youth - ca 0AG

Only a few days after the events at the Alrose and Elerose farms, the adventurers find themselves once again called to action. While drinking in the local tavern, a young boy named Walcott Norion arrived in the farm claiming his friend Mathius Ballister was in grave danger at someplace called "the honeycomb" after a bear attack. Though they questioned his story, they did believe the bit about his friend being in danger, and agreed to help. They traveled to the Honeycomb and discovered evidence of a serious injury and the boy gone, apparently having staggered away to the east.

His trail ultimately was picked up by some goblins who apparently picked him up and headed home. Following their trail was easy, and the band had the help of a runty goblin named Plyx, who informed them he was from a nearby abandonded keep which had recently been visited by a giant named Beszar who appointed himself king of the erstwhile independent goblin clan. Finding the keep was not difficult, though getting in and moving through it's trapped halls proved daunting, sapping the health and strength of the adventurers.

So it was they came to Beszar's "throne room," where he lounged with a great snake coiled about his arms, in a weakend state. Though they fought valiantly, killing the serpent and leaving Beszar greivously injured, all appeared lost for the adventurers as they fell one by one to his wicked claws or the venom of his pet. Yet, as the cowardly Krenurt, who had been taking refuge in the entry to the chamber, and who had been completely overlookd by Beszar, watched the last of his companions fall, anger burned inside of him. Before he was even truly aware of what he was doing, he had knocked and loosed an arrow from his tiny bow, an arrow which found its mark in one of Bezar's grotesquely deformed ears. The giant's arm, raised to strike a killing blow against Balec, fell limply to his side, he took a few steps and turned toward Krenurt, a look of bafflement in his eyes, just before he toppled to the ground dead.

After this somewhat inauspicious victory, the adventurers returned to Nairen after recovering the comatose body of Mathius Ballister and delivering it to Ardrenai.

Sidebar: The King in Red - ca 0AG

As the group continued to wait for the arrival of Silion, the holiday of Sudoc drew near. Two-Bridges, the town Balec and Rhogar call home, is known to have a very lively Sudoc festival. The adventurers, along with several locals and lumbermen decided to head to the village to join in the festivities. They befriended a woman named Ashny, her fiance Issv, and her mother Tarily along the way. There was much drinking and merriment on the first day of the journey.

A horde of zombies emerged from the jungle in the middle of the night on the first night of the journey. Several people were killed or dragged away, and though the adventurers were able to save several people as well as staying alive themselves, the mood of the journey became sour. In the morning, all but a handful had departed, and the adventurers continued on toward Grimborough. It would not be until they returned from that city that they would realize they'd never been headed to Grimborough in the first place.

Upon arriving in Grimborough on Sudoc's Eve, Issv and Ashny departed, telling the adventurers they planned to head to a tavern they owned called the Dead Jester, and that they could come see them later. From that moment on, everything became a nightmare. It turned out that they had not come to the Grimborough they knew, but a sort of twisted version of the city in a place called Tsar Noar, the domain of the fabled Baloch, the King in Red. One horrific event piled upon another in the dreadful realm, and the adventurers barely escaped with their lives and sanity in-tact. Though their memories of their time in Tsar Noar sometimes feel like a dream, the axe now carried by Rhogar, those still missing from Nairen and Two-Bridges, and the eerie presence of the shadowstone all serve as reminders that the events therein were all too real.

Part Three: The Man in Purple - ca 0AG

Though usually a time of merriment, the normally raucous Sudoc holiday was muted in Nairen and Two Bridges following the disappearance of several townsfolk and lumbermen, as well as the return of the group with the corpse of Ashny. In addition, raids by goblins, undead sightings, and other strange occurrences were on the rise in Nairen, keeping Anissa and the few guards in the town busy at all hours of day and night. As the group dragged themselves down into the common room of the Greenbriar after the final night of Sudoc celebrations, they were greeted with a message from Handel that Anissa had been by earlier in the morning, and asked that they meet her at her office.

Tired, and perhaps a little hung over, the group ate breakfast then departed to visit the sheriff. There, she asked them to accompany her to the Emory farm and help her escort the family back to Nairen. She gave little in the way of explanation other than she believed that the family was in danger and that the town guards were all out on other business.

After some discussion the group agreed to accompany her, encountering first a small company of bandits that were subdued and left to be collected later. They then continued on to find the Dremmel farm had been the site of some grisly murder, and was now infested with living shadows. After a very hard-won battle against the apparitions, Anissa agreed to rest while the group recovered their strength before continuing onto the Emory farm. She had a frank conversation with the group at the time, grudgingly acknowledging that, despite her misgivings, they had been a boon to the small village and helped in a time of growing troubles. She offered to deputize the group and offer them the deed to a small homestead that had been abandoned during the Sudoc disappearances. The writ would need to be accompanied by an official title, and the group settled on Krenurt's Crew in honor of the goblin who they had saved and who, in return, had saved them.

After spending a couple hours resting up and conversing, the group continued onto the Emory farm. There they were assaulted by a mysterious man in purple, who alluded to a master named Vanya and placed a curse upon Anissa. The group fought off the undead and the necromancer, who ultimately summoned a horrific beast of blood and sinew to chase down Beatrice Emory. Krenurt's Crew split, with Rhogar and Krenurt hanging back to mop up the undead while the rest chased the beast, catching and dispatching it just before it was able to kill Beatrice Emory. After returning to the home, Abraham took custody of his injured wife and though he was grateful at first, the group became a bit hostile toward Beatrice and the family and ultimately returned to Nairen with Anissa only, intending to come back and question the Emory family after she recovered.

It was not to be, however, for when they returned to Nairen and brought Anissa to Ardrenai they were tasked with gathering a magical component from the mountains to the north. She had used the remainder of it tending to the Walcott boy, and feared that Anissa would require constant care to prevent her condition from deteriorating while she awaited the return of the Crew with the pure water. Thus the group was once again rushed into yet another adventure, with little time for rest or recuperation.
