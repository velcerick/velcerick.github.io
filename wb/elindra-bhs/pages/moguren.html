Moguren

  • Parent: Player Options

From the patch of detritus and fungus, a humanoid figure rose, wearing armor cobbled together from scraps of metal and bits of wood bound with vine. In one hand it held a rusted, but well-oiled and sharpened dagger, its other hand empty and held out before it. I spoke to the thing, though it lacked a mouth to reply. Instead, it shook its head, releasing a cloud of spores which settled upon my skin. They left me with a vague sensation, like blurred thoughts. To this day I think the thing was merely trying to communicate with me. Unfortunately, Sir Denbar took the action as hostile, and cut the creature down where it stood. I’ve not seen its like since.

- from The Voyages of Den Sundaril

Moguren are a reclusive race of humanoid fungi occasionally found in remote forests, swamps, and damp caverns. Although each moguren colony is unique, the race shares some common characteristics. Among scholars of such things, moguren are known for finding inventive uses of scavenged and recycled materials in their architecture and craftsmanship. Further, their alchemists create potions, powders, tonics, and elixirs unique to their race, which are coveted by those who know of them.

Fungal Variety

Moguren vary as widely in their physical appearance as the types of fungus that can be found in the environment where their colony thrives. In general, they have a humanoid shape, like mushrooms with arms and legs. However, alien-looking varieties of moguren, including bioluminescent, amorphous, puffy, or many-limbed individuals are not unheard of.

Colonies and Circles

Moguren are organized into isolated colonies, ranging from a few dozen members to massive supercolonies. Most have a structure similar to insect hives, where each individual has a specific role and a circle of elder moguren act as the brains of the colony. Although this structure appears very mechanical to outsiders, individual moguren actually have a higher degree of autonomy and independent thought than is obvious to outsiders.

Most moguren colonies are completely isolated from other friendly humanoids. However, if contact is made, moguren are not opposed to interacting with outsiders. Their natural curiosity and interest in manufactured goods occasionally leads to open lines of communication and trade if language barriers can be overcome.

Moguren have a naturally inquisitive nature, which lends itself well to adventure. However, their naivety about the world outside of their colonies means most adventuresome moguren find an early death. Further, few have the skills needed to survive independently.

Language

Language is perhaps the largest impediment to the greater integration of moguren into typical society. They communicate with one another completely non-verbally through the dissemination and “reading” of spores which, to other races, is meaningless and may even come across as hostility. Scattered colonies which have had regular interactions with outsiders have been known to develop rudimentary speech. There have also been some successful efforts made among moguren alchemists to create tonics that allow creatures with exposed skin to absorb moguren speech spores in order to obtain a rudimentary understanding of spore speech

Scavengers

Moguren are scavengers in many things. They are known to find uses for lost and discarded items they come across, their clothing, armor, weapons, and furniture typically cobbled together from bits of wood and stone they have found rather than harvested.

However, the moguren are also scavengers of ideas. For instance, it remains unclear whether moguren have any religion of their own. Despite this, some moguren colonies and individual moguren have taken up the worship of deities introduced by outsiders, and old colonies have been seen found wearing the trappings of and performing the rites of deities otherwise long forgotten. Furthermore, in rare instances where a moguren colony has had a history of contact with outsiders their regular hierarchal structure tends to break down and family units emerge. Some colonies have been found to have adopted the cultural norms of other humanoid civilizations wholesale.

Moguren Names

Within a typical colony, moguren do not adopt individual names. To outsiders they often take names which reflect how the outsider views them. In the rare colonies where self-identification is the norm, a naming scheme is usually somewhat normalized. They might use sequential words from an important book, physically descriptive names, numerical or symbolic names, or names related to a circle. These schemes are unique to each colony.

Outsider Names. Fungiman, Lycoperdon, Morchalt, Puffer, Shroomy, Toad

Colony Names. 15, And, Bluecap, First, Joseph, Speaker, Warrior, Y

Playing a Moguren

In order to select the Moguren race, you must have completed the Book of Terniel adventure path from Embers Design Studios. If you did not participate, you must gain advance permission from your DM to play, as the race does not strictly follow the standard balancing rules for playable races.

Playing a Moguren

In order to select the Moguren race, you must have completed the Book of Terniel adventure path from Embers Design Studios. If you did not participate, you must gain advance permission from your DM to play, as the race does not strictly follow the standard balancing rules for playable races.

Moguren Traits

Your moguren character has the following racial traits.

Ability Score Modifications. Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Charisma score decreases by 1. Moguren are wise, but difficult to relate to.

Age. Moguren have no set lifespan. As plants, they can live for as long as they continue to find food and water. Within a colony, moguren usually develop individual consciousness after a few weeks of life.

Alignment. Moguren are a generally benign race occupied primarily with the preservation and advancement of the colony. This focus on self-preservation is a neutral endeavor. Some moguren colonies become xenophobic and violent, killing outsiders on sight. Other moguren colonies have developed a desire to expand, and will do so at the expense of other intelligent races in their way. Such violent colonies are more likely to develop underground.

Darkvision. You can see in dim light within 30 feet of you as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light. You can't discern color in darkness, only shades of gray.

Fungoid. You do not bleed and have advantage on death saving throws.  Rather than consuming food and drink through a bodily orifice, you absorb food and water through pores in your body, leaving behind a dry husk.

Living Plant. You count as both a plant and a humanoid for spells and affects related to creature type. You have advantage on saving throws against mind-affecting effects, paralysis, poison, and disease.

You breathe and eat, but do not need to sleep. To gain the benefits of a long rest you merely need to spend the requisite time in a period of inactivity. While you do not grow exhausted from extended periods of exertion, you do need to consume twice the normal amount of food and water to remain active during this time.

Scavenger. Select one type of artisan’s tools of your choice. You can craft items which would normally require proficiency in these tools without them. Instead of paying half the regular cost when crafting these items, you only need to pay one third of the regular cost. 

Sporespeech. You can communicate with other moguren using sporespeech. If you are proficient in alchemist’s tools, you can spend 10gp worth of materials and one downtime day to craft a potion which will allow a single humanoid to be able to receive basic communication from your spores for 24 hours.

Spore Cloud. As a standard action, you can emit a cloud of spores. At character creation, select one of the spore types below. For harmful spores, the save DC against your spores is 8 + Constitution Modifier + proficiency bonus. After you use this ability, it cannot be used again until you take a short or long rest.

Sleep Spores. Creatures adjacent to the moguren are affected as though within the radius of a sleep spell cast by the moguren. The number of hit points affected increases at odd numbered levels as though cast using a higher level spell slot.

Blinding Spores. Creatures adjacent to the moguren are blinded for 1d4 rounds. A successful Constitution saving throw negates the effect.

Noxious Spores. The moguren emits a cloud of noxious spores that persist for 1d4 rounds. Creatures adjacent to the moguren have disadvantage on attacks against the moguren for the duration of the effect.

Soothing Spores. The moguren emits a cloud of soothing spores. Creatures adjacent to the moguren are healed a number of hit points equal to the moguren’s Proficiency Bonus + Constitution Modifier.

Language. You begin play with sporespeech and one other language of your choice.

Subraces

Moguren come in a myriad of shapes and sizes. Select one of the following when creating your moguren PC.

Builders

The builders are essentially the rank and file moguren. They are no more or less likely to hold any particular position within a colony than any other moguren. Their physical characteristics vary widely as described above.

Ability Score Increase. Your constitution score increases by 1. Most moguren are tough and heal more easily than regular humanoids.

Size. You can elect to be small or medium sized. This choice has no impact on any other traits or abilities.

Speed. Your base walking speed is 25 feet. Moguren are ponderous creatures.

Master Scavengers. Select two additional types of artisan’s tools for use with your scavenger ability. You may choose herbalism kits instead of artisan’s tools.

Sporesowers

Sporesower moguren are typically small and have abnormally yielding flesh full of spores. In most moguren colonies sporesowers are usually in charge of germination and spore spreading. They tend to be naturally friendly and open-minded, but they lack the focus and physical agility of other moguren. Sporesower moguren are some of the most outlandish in appearance. While some have a mostly humanoid trunk accented or covered with puffy growths, others are simply single puff balls with dangling arms and legs.

Size. Your spowersower moguren is small sized, and may stand anywhere from two to four feet tall.

Speed. You are slower than others of your kind. You have a base walking speed of 20 feet.

Puff. You can elect to use your spore cloud ability as a reaction in response to taking damage instead of a standard action. 

Yielding Flesh. You have resistance to bludgeoning and falling damage.

Hollowguard

The Hollowguard are an entirely distinct subspecies of moguren with thin, wrinkly flesh surrounding a hollow interior. Stronger than other moguren, they are occasionally referred to as brain moguren by outsiders, a name that comes not from their intellect, but rather from flesh that has a distinctly creased and wrinkled appearance similar to the folds in a brain. Most moguren colonies do not include circles of hollowguards, and when they do spawn they naturally acclimate to a guardian role, and usually keep themselves somewhat apart from the rest of the colony. Unlike most moguren, the hollowguard tend to be suspicious of outsiders, and lack some of the inquisitive and open nature of their brethren. 

Ability Score Increase. Your strength score increases by 1. 

Size. You are medium sized. Hollowguard tend toward the upper end of medium size, usually standing between six and eight feet tall, but rarely weigh more than 30 or 40 pounds.

Speed. Hollowguard are taller and more mobile than most  other moguren. You have a base walking speed of 30 feet.

Hollow Flesh. Your hollowguard moguren lacks any internal anatomy and has non-porous flesh. You are resistant to poison damage and immune to the poisoned condition.
