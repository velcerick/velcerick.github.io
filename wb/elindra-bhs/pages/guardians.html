Guardians

“But yer a guardian! You belong ta the goblins. They’ll be coming fer ya sure as Saeth’s lies, and that’ll mean trouble fer us,” barked the old man between swills of his ale.

Handel watched the exchange warily. He knew old Abel was just a harmless drunk, and believed that Garun wanted little more than to play his cittern. Still, the things were made – awakened he mentally corrected himself – for violence.

Across the table, the stony features of the brightly painted statue remained placid, though a sudden grinding and rasping sound suggested tension to Handel. A cool, musically hollow note echoed from the guardian’s mouth, perhaps a sigh, and his posture seemed to relax. A patient smile touched his lips as he spoke again, “as I have said, I belong to no one. The goblins… encourage us… to work for them, but we have our own will. My will is to entertain, perhaps another ale will put your mind at rest.”

Handel chuckled as he saw Abel’s face light up, as if he didn’t drink nearly a constant stream of ale all the days of his life anymore. The two continued to banter, and Handel let go his worry about the clay musician.

- a meeting at the Greenbriar Inn

The guardians are a race of beings that were brought into existence by the Southland goblins. Most Elindrans, having never met one, believe them to be little more than automatons or golems, similar to the creatures that the gnomes create. This viewpoint is wrong. Although the goblins create and awaken the guardians, and although they often serve the goblins, many are awakened with a strong spark of independence and strike out on their own. Goblin and guardian pairings typically develop more as friendship or loose partnership than a master and slave relationship.

Statuesque

A guardian begins existence as a roughly humanoid clay statue between 4 and 10 feet tall. Some guardians retain this initial shape, those believing it to be their ‘natural’ form. More commonly, a guardian will choose to be carved into some shape based on the personality and mannerisms it has adopted. These shapes are always generally humanoid, with two legs and two arms, but otherwise range as widely as the humanoids that exist with the rare guardian choosing a shape drawn wholly from its own imagination. These shapes may be carven naked or clothed, embellished with real fur, hair, and feathers or carven facsimiles of such.  It is all the will of the creator or created. Without staining or other coloration, most guardians have a terracotta red coloration, as the clays they are most often forged from are that color. Some guardians have alternate colorations being forged from clay or mud of a different composition, or having been dyed prior to awakening. Many dye, paint, or lacquer their “flesh” after awakening.

The process of awakening a guardian (see sidebar Ritual of Awakening) requires the use of specifically placed and shaped magical stones to animate and keep the guardian alive and connected to a soul line. All guardians have a circular disc of polished stone, three inches in diameter, set into their forehead. These discs are made from a piece of black and a piece of white stone designed to interlock in a pattern unique to each guardian. Similarly, guardians all have eyes that are polished clear orbs, but which glow while the guardian lives with a color reflective of its personality or mood. The remaining three stones are identical in color and shape on all guardians: an egg sized and shaped red gemstone set between their collarbones; amber ovoid stones set into the soles of their feet; and cylindrical blue bars, about an inch long, on the underside of each forearm.

Ritual of Awakening

The ancient spirit magic of the Southland goblins, used even before they evolved beyond their squabbling primitive roots, is the basis for the ritual of awakening. These roots are evident in the preparation of the vessel, a mass of clay, mixed with secret alchemical fluids lain out in a roughly humanoid shape. New to the process is the inclusion of elarius, gemstones with inherent magical properties aligned to the cardinal elements. These stones are placed in a very specific configuration: a fiery red egg at the base of the throat; a pair of blue-green rods inset in the forearms; matching amber ovoids set into the soles of each foot; glassy clear orbs for the eyes, and a disc of interlocking white and black stone set into the forehead. It is these stones that allow the awakening to take place, to create in the guardians a permanence where the ancient magic was a fleeting thing.

After preparing the vessel, the shamans perform a complex arcane ritual. There is much dancing and chanting, ritual offerings to the spirits, and the burning of incense. When it is finished, assuming the magic is set, a deep light begins to glow in the eyes of the guardian, and a line opens for its mouth. Much of the clay firms to a semi-firm, dry state, the rest sloughing off as the guardian rises, alive and self-aware.

Independent Souls

During the ritual of awakening, a humanoid soul is drawn into the prepared vessel, and a new guardian is born. Newly awakened guardians have full control of their motor functions; the ability to read, write, and speak at least a single language; a working knowledge of the world; and even some specialized skills and background knowledge. Conversely, as it did not exist prior to its awakening, the guardian has no memory, no self-identity, and no personality besides perhaps some vestige of the natural racial tendencies of their soul of origin.

Despite this blank slate, the guardians tend to be highly independent. Early on, the goblins tried to force this trait from them using magical binding, torture, conditioning, and other training. However, this resulted in violent uprisings and the deaths of many guardians and goblins alike. Over the centuries since the advent of the ritual, the goblins have refined the process for tying the guardians to them, moving toward subtlety and manipulation and away from coercion and force. They allow those who wish to leave the Southlands, though most choose to stay.

Most guardians who choose to leave the Southlands value their independence and freedom above all else. It is rare to find a guardian that chooses to leave the South only to live a life in the service of others. Instead, most guardians choose independent professions as business owners, adventurers, mercenaries, or other positions which allow them to be self-sufficient and not reliant or servile in any direct way.

Homeless Wanderers

Guardians have no true homeland. Though there are some historic accounts of guardians attempting to form their own society or band together to overthrow the goblins, the lack of any ability to procreate or even any real racial unity has doomed such efforts to failure. Because of this, and because the goblins have learned the value of keeping the bonds they place upon the guardians light and nearly invisible, most who leave the Southlands wander. While some have a clear purpose, most simply go out to find or forge their identity, to learn what they can of the world. Many even return to the Southlands after a time, unable to find true purpose in a world so driven by the pursuits of organic living things.

Guardian Names

In the Southlands, it is tradition for a guardian to work with a goblin partner to come up with a name on the first anniversary of their awakening. These are often words in the goblin or the guardian’s native tongue that relate to a specific skill or area of expertise. Wandering guardians can have any name, from a typical name to a descriptive one.

Male Names: Aurak, Bendar, Book, Colin, Charger, Fen, Garulin, Hardscrabble, Kendillian, Rockface, Shendithinor, Weaver

Female Names: Crysta, Dogrunner, Faralishin, Fraali, Gorger, Hera, Holorishantha, J, Knife, Mara, Nebran, Quarali, Runner

What is Elarius?

Elarius is a gemstone with inherent magical properties. Mythology posits that elarius stones were used to draw the elemental energies of creation into the world, and those that remain behind were remnants meant for man to discover on their own.  Lothiath's gift to man gave them magic much earlier, and elarius went long undiscovered outside of very specialized uses, mostly in isolated civilizations and tribal cultures. There are six known varieties of elarius: clear air elarius, amber earth elarius, aquamarine water elarius, red fire elarius, black spirit elarius, and white void elarius. Elarius use is a subtle art, requiring precise geometry in carving and application to be used successfully and the stones are rare. Spirit elarius in particular, which allows for the intermixing of elemental forces, is especially difficult to find in Elindra. Mana is generally considered a more versatile and easier to access form of magic, and is much more widely utilized.

Guardian Traits

Your guardian character has the following racial traits.

Ability Score Modifications. Your Constitution score increases by 1.

Age. Guardians awaken fully developed, though they have no personal identity. Though their physical body only ages through the development of chips, cracks, and mars which can be repaired, the magic within them fades over time. Most guardians live between 300 and 400 years before they stop functioning altogether.

Alignment. Guardians who remain in the southlands tend toward lawful alignments. Other guardians do not have a proclivity toward a specific alignment. However, goblins who exhibit distinctly evil traits in their first weeks of existence are killed as failures.

Size. Guardians stand anywhere from 4 to 10 feet tall and are as heavy as an equal volume of fired clay. Your size is either small or medium, although PC guardians can be no taller than 8 feet.

Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.

Soulborn Construct. You are a construct, though you have a humanoid soul. As a construct you do not need to eat, drink, or breathe, though you can drink and breathe if you choose to. You are immune to disease and poison. Your elarius sustains you, but is used up throughout the course of the day. If you do not rest for at least six hours per day, your eyes dim to a dull glow, you gain levels of exhaustion as normal. As a creature with a soul, you dream as normal creatures do.  

Elarius Dependency. You are not kept alive by organic processes, relying instead on magic fueled by the stones set into the various parts of your body. When injured, blood does not flow, but ephemeral vapors seep from the wound as the magic burns to heal you. You are not affected by non-magical healing and cannot be stabilized using a Medicine check. If you are dying, you can be stabilized with a DC 10 Intelligence (Arcana) check or the mending spell.

Further, if you become separated from your elarius through dismemberment or some other means, you immediately go inert, the light in your eyes winking out. Your soul will linger in your vessel for 1d4 weeks after such a separation. You can only be restored to consciousness if your elarius is replaced, requiring a successful Intelligence (Arcana) check DC 20. A southland goblin shaman has advantage on this check.

Language. You can speak, read, and write common, goblin, and up to one other language of your choice. This third language reflects your origin.

Subtypes

When the guardians were first created, they were considered tools of battle, and given names based on qualities related to battle. Though they are no longer thought of as merely tools, the old monikers have hung on. Your guardian character can be one of the following subtypes. 

Arrow

Arrow guardians were created as scouts, spies, and archers. Their bodies retain some moisture and malleability, making them more flexible than normal humanoids. 

Ability Score Increase. Your Dexterity score increases by 1.

Size. Arrow guardians are always small sized, typically standing between 3 and 4 feet tall.

Malleable Form. Your flesh retains some malleability, allowing you to bend and move in ways that might be impossible for others. You have advantage on Dexterity (Acrobatics) checks and a climb speed of 20.

Hammer

Hammer guardians were created with offense in mind. Their limbs are denser and tougher, allowing them to strike fearsome blows even when unarmed.

Ability Score Increase. Your Strength score increases by 1.

Size. Hammer guardians are always medium sized, typically standing between 6 and 8 feet tall with a broad stature.

Stonefist. You have learned to use your body as a weapon. When unarmed you can make a devastating slam attack using a full body punch or both of your fists which deals bludgeoning damage equal to 1d10 + your Strength modifier. If you are carrying a weapon, you can use your bonus action to strike with another limb, your head, or an open hand to deal damage equal to 1d4 + your Strength modifier. These attacks are considered melee weapon attacks with which you are proficient. 

Shield

By far the most common type of guardian, shields were designed with defense in mind. Their flesh is as hard and stony as kiln-fired clay, making them more difficult to damage than other guardians. 

Ability Score Increase. Your Constitution score increases by 1.

Stony Exterior. Your skin is tougher than that of most guardians. When unarmored, your AC is 14 + your Dexterity modifier. If wearing armor, you choose the better of your natural armor or your manufactured armor when determining AC. You can also use a shield while unarmored, gaining its full benefit.
